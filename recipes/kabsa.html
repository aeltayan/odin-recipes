Kabsa

Kabsa is a vibrant and aromatic rice dish commonly enjoyed across the Arabian Peninsula, particularly in Saudi Arabia. It's known for its rich blend of spices and diverse ingredients, making it a hearty and flavorful meal. Typically, Kabsa features basmati rice cooked with a fragrant mix of spices such as saffron, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. The dish often includes a protein element, like chicken, lamb, or beef, which is braised or roasted and then mixed with the rice. Vegetables like tomatoes, carrots, and bell peppers can also be added for extra flavor and texture.

Kabsa is garnished with fried onions, raisins, and nuts like almonds or cashews, adding a delightful crunch and a hint of sweetness. The dish is usually served with a side of salad or yogurt, enhancing its flavor profile. The combination of spices, tender meat, and perfectly cooked rice creates a dish that’s both comforting and indulgent, making Kabsa a beloved staple in Middle Eastern cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Rice (basmati or long-grain)
  • Protein (chicken, lamb, or beef)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes
  • Carrots
  • Bell Peppers
  • Mixed spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, cardamom, cloves)
  • Broth (chicken or vegetable)
  • Vegetable oil or ghee
  • Raisins or sultanas
  • Nuts (almonds or cashews)
  • Salt
  • Optional garnishes (fried onions, fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse 2 cups of basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak for about 30 minutes if you have time, then drain.
  2. Cook the Protein:
    1. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat.
    2. Add 1-2 pounds of chicken, lamb, or beef (cut into pieces). Brown the meat on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics:
    • In the same pot, add 1 large chopped onion and 2 cloves of minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add Vegetables and Spices:
    1. Stir in 1-2 diced tomatoes, 1-2 diced carrots, and 1-2 chopped bell peppers.
    2. Add mixed spices (like cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, cardamom, and cloves) to taste.
    3. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are softened and the spices are fragrant.
  5. Combine and Simmer:
    1. Return the browned meat to the pot.
    2. Stir in 1 tablespoon of tomato paste (optional) and 4 cups of broth (chicken or vegetable).
    3. Add 1-2 bay leaves and 1-2 cinnamon sticks.
    4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer until the meat is cooked through and tender (about 30-40 minutes for chicken, longer for lamb or beef).
  6. Cook the Rice:
    1. Once the meat is cooked, add the drained rice to the pot.
    2. Stir to combine and ensure the rice is submerged in the liquid.
    3. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).
  7. Garnish and Serve:
    1. Fluff the rice with a fork and stir in 1/4 cup raisins or sultanas and toasted nuts (like almonds or cashews).
    2. Garnish with fried onions and chopped fresh herbs (like parsley or cilantro) if desired.
  8. Serve Hot:
    • Serve the Kabsa hot, ideally with a side of yogurt or a fresh salad.
